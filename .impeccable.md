## Design Context

### Users

Credit union CEOs and senior board members at cooperatives in the $200M to $3B asset range, evaluating whether to hire Brent Dixon as a strategic facilitator (planning, workshops, coaching). They are not buying software. They are deciding whether to trust one person with a strategy conversation that matters to their organization.

Secondary audience: industry peers (other facilitators, researchers, Filene/CUNA/NASCUS adjacent staff) who may refer Brent, book him to speak, or subscribe to his newsletter. AI agents summarizing the site on a CEO's behalf are a deliberate third audience (see `llms.txt`).

Context of use: most visits come from a referral, a LinkedIn click, a newsletter footer, or an event introduction. The visitor is already warm. They are checking whether Brent looks real, serious, and like a person they would want in the room. The site is a credibility proof, not a lead-generation funnel.

Primary job: decide to reach out or subscribe to the newsletter. Secondary job: skim the framework and services well enough to mention Brent accurately to a colleague.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **confident, warm, direct.**

Voice: first-person, conversational, rhythmic. Treats the reader as a peer, not a prospect. No corporate jargon, no growth-hacker verbs ("dominate," "crush," "disrupt," "unlock"), no AI-vendor triumphalism. The site should feel like a person, not a firm.

Emotional goals: the reader should feel respected, intellectually engaged, and slightly relieved. Relieved because most consultant sites in this space either overreach ("we will transform your future") or hide behind jargon. This one does neither.

Anti-tone: agency bravado, fintech shine, generic McKinsey gloss, or any flavor of "disruptor energy." Also explicitly not: CUCollaborate, Alloy Labs, or other peer-firm visual languages that lean on Montserrat, icon-above-heading cards, or purple-blue gradient blooms.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Swiss Grid, updated.** Explicitly built on Josef Müller-Brockmann's principle that design should have "the clearly intelligible, objective, functional, and aesthetic quality of mathematical thinking." Dark by default with a light theme toggle. White space is designed, not leftover. Asymmetry happens through varied column spans inside grid constraints, not free-floating placement.

Theme: dark default. A credit union CEO evaluating Brent on a laptop at 9pm after board prep should find a surface that signals depth and focus. Warm accent colors (gold, coral, teal) prevent the dark from reading cold or corporate. Light mode is a toggle, not the primary surface.

Typography: Plus Jakarta Sans (display), Inter (body), Fraunces (editorial pull quotes). All self-hosted. No Montserrat (peer firm already owns it). No Satoshi / Karla / Lora (retired v3.0). No Inter-only monoculture.

Color: gold (#FBE248), coral (#CF5A5A), teal (#529099 dark / #2D5A66 light) over warm off-white or near-black neutrals. CTA buttons use coral in light mode, gold/teal in dark. Do not reintroduce indigo.

Signature interactive moves (preserve unless explicitly retired): grainy gradient hero bloom with SVG noise, three p5.js scroll-driven sketches in services, three more in the framework section, custom cursor with follower on desktop, continuous logo marquee in clients strip.

Reference points: Müller-Brockmann grid posters, editorial magazines (Monocle, The Browser, Offscreen), Bret Victor's writing layouts. Anti-references: CUCollaborate, most fintech agency sites, AI-startup landing pages with purple-blue blooms and icon cards.

### Design Principles

1. **The grid is load-bearing.** Every element aligns to the 8px baseline. Buttons are square-cornered on purpose. Asymmetry through column spans is the signature move, not free placement. When in doubt, respect the grid.

2. **White space is a design element, not leftover.** Sections breathe at 96 to 128px vertical rhythm. Paragraphs cap at 70ch. A button sits in its own column of space. Add space before you add ornament.

3. **Warm the seriousness.** Dark surfaces, precise typography, and mathematical spacing could easily read cold. The gold, coral, and teal accents (plus Fraunces italics for the outcome quote) are the counterweight. Never let either side win alone.

4. **Feel like a person, not a firm.** First-person voice in copy. Personal photography, not stock. Signature interactive flourishes that come from Brent's taste (p5.js sketches, custom cursor, grainy bloom) rather than off-the-shelf component kits. A reader should be able to tell a human decided every element.

5. **Respect the reader's time.** Every section must earn its scroll. No redundant headers. No hero-metric templates. No three-card-grid filler. If a block does not advance the CEO toward "reach out" or "subscribe," it is noise.

## Standing Rules (derived from CLAUDE.md and DESIGN.md)

- Never use em dashes anywhere in the interface or in prose about the interface. Periods, commas, parentheses, or restructure.
- Never use border-left or border-right greater than 1px as a colored accent stripe (impeccable global ban, reinforced here).
- Never use gradient text (`background-clip: text` on gradients).
- Never nest cards inside cards. Never wrap everything in cards.
- Never use generic stock imagery. Photography is personal.
- Never remove the 2px accent-gold focus outline. Accessibility requirement.
- Square-corner buttons (radius 0) are a Swiss-grid signature. Do not round them.
- Body text caps at 70ch. Hard rule.
- All spacing is a multiple of 8px. No arbitrary values.
- Teal is the primary interactive color. Always.
- Section-label h2s go uppercase with 0.1em letter-spacing. Preserve.
