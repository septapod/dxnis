# If We Had to Launch in One Hour

What I would change on the live site right now, ranked by impact.

## Cut immediately

1. **"Strategic facilitation for credit unions in the age of agentic AI" as the hero positioning line.** The research says CU buyers do not search for "strategic facilitation" and "in the age of agentic AI" is our framing, not theirs. Replace with something that names the pain they already feel. Draft: "I help credit union leaders build strategy that survives Monday morning." Or: "Strategic planning for credit unions that actually gets used." The AI angle belongs in the newsletter and the about section, not the first thing a cold visitor reads.

2. **Any remaining process language in the services section.** "Multi-session engagements," "discovery process," anything that describes how the sausage is made. Replace with what the client walks away with. Traci (the buyer persona) does not care about your process. She cares about whether her board will think this was worth the money.

3. **The word "facilitation" in the hero and services headings.** It is accurate but it undersells. George Hofheimer calls himself a "strategy advisor" and sells roles ("The Chief Strategy Officer," "The Outside Perspective"). "Facilitation" sounds like someone who runs a meeting. Brent designs strategy engagements, measures alignment, builds interactive deliverables, and stays on to help implement. "Facilitation" does not cover that.

## Rewrite immediately

4. **The three service descriptions.** Right now they are thin. Each one needs three things:
   - One sentence naming the situation the buyer is in (not a generic problem, a specific moment: "You are two years into a plan that no longer fits your reality")
   - One sentence saying what they walk away with (concrete: "A strategy-on-a-page your board can read in five minutes and your team can execute all year")
   - One sentence that separates this from the default ("Before we enter the room, I measure where your leadership team agrees and disagrees, so we spend time on decisions, not rehearsing consensus")

5. **The beliefs section.** The current three beliefs are directionally right but need sharpening with the raw material from the interview. The strongest candidate belief from the answers: "Most credit unions do not have a strategy. They have a plan. There is a difference, and it shows up six months later when the plan is sitting in a drawer." This is provocative, true, and backed by Filene's own research on the implementation gap.

6. **The "How I Work" phases.** They are currently abstract. With the interview material, Phase 1 can now say something concrete: "I start by measuring where your leadership team is aligned and where they are not. This shapes everything that happens next." Phase 3 can say: "You do not get a deck that lives in a file folder. You get a strategy-on-a-page and tools your team uses all year."

## Add immediately

7. **A concrete outcome line somewhere above the fold.** The competitors who win business lead with outcomes. Cornerstone: "$1.5M tangible return per $1B in assets." On The Mark: "25% new member growth." Brent does not have dollar figures yet, but he has testimonial language: "The best strategic discussion we have ever had." Use that as a pull quote near the hero. It is proof, not a claim.

8. **The newsletter as a primary credibility signal.** The research says 75% of B2B decision-makers explore services after encountering thought leadership. The newsletter section should be higher on the page or referenced in the hero area. Not "subscribe to my newsletter" but "Every week, I write about what is changing for credit unions. 70+ CU leaders read it." The subscriber count is small but the audience quality is high.

9. **A "Who this is for" signal.** Sarah (the league president) and Traci (the CU exec) need to see themselves in the first scroll. Something like: "I work with credit union CEOs and boards navigating strategic transitions, whether that is a ground-up strategy build, a mid-cycle reset, or a pivot that changes everything." This is from the interview answers. Three real use cases, three real client situations (anonymized).

## Do not touch

- The visual design, color palette, typography, and interactive sketches. These are strong and differentiated. Ship as-is.
- The about section bio. It needs work but it is not the bottleneck. A cold visitor who gets to the bio is already interested.
- The /agents page and llms.txt. Nice to have, not blocking launch.
- Dark mode. It works. Do not tinker.

## The one-hour priority order

If I had exactly 60 minutes:

1. **(15 min)** Rewrite the hero H1 and subline to name the buyer's pain, not our methodology. Cut "strategic facilitation" and "agentic AI" from the headline.
2. **(20 min)** Rewrite the three service descriptions with situation/outcome/differentiator structure.
3. **(10 min)** Add a pull-quote testimonial line near the hero.
4. **(10 min)** Rewrite the "How I Work" phase descriptions with concrete language from the interview.
5. **(5 min)** Update the beliefs section with one sharper statement from the interview material.

Everything else can wait for the next session after Brent finishes the interview questions.
