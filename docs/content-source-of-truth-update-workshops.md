### What makes his sessions different (from Q8)

Four differentiators Brent named:

1. **Emotional connection first.** Data and information are "nicely nestled in stories and narratives that connect emotionally, which in turn allows the information to connect cognitively as well. The emotional connection has to happen first."

2. **Humor and entertainment.** "I include a lot of humor. I make jokes. I make people laugh. I insist that it is entertaining from the get-go."

3. **Interactive by default.** Even in keynotes and general sessions, Brent creates time for discussion, for people to "get their hands dirty, to work together, begin exploring a concept beyond just hearing about it."

4. **Visually compelling.** Design background means strong visual presentations. "A big part of what ends up making some of this stuff feel quite accessible."

**The competitor contrast (Brent named it directly):**
A competitor delivered a session to the same audience before Brent. The feedback: "very academic, very theoretical, and they walked away being like, hmm, interesting, but didn't know how to act on it." Brent was brought in specifically to bridge that gap between understanding and action.

### Why boards and teams book private sessions (from Q9)

Four real use cases:

1. **All-staff innovation tone-setting.** An organization wanted to frame innovation and efficiency for their entire staff. Brent used the experimentation topic (1,000 tiny experiments) to connect innovation to risk reduction and efficiency.

2. **From theory to action.** After a previous presenter was too academic, Brent was hired to help the executive team get hands-dirty with agentic AI and feel confident enough to act.

3. **Board 101 sessions.** Boards are volunteer, sometimes retired, often a few steps removed from daily operations. They need fast-tracked foundational sessions on emerging technology or strategy. More boards than executive teams request this.

4. **Brain food groundwork.** One client brought Brent in specifically to lay an intellectual foundation so their own facilitator could lead a meaningful discussion afterward. Brent as the catalyst, someone else as the facilitator.

## New Tensions

### Tension 5: The "theory to action" bridge
Brent's workshops are explicitly positioned against competitors who are "very academic, very theoretical." His audiences want to walk away able to act. The site copy for workshops should lead with this: you leave able to do something.

### Tension 6: Board vs. executive audience
Boards and executive teams have different needs and different baselines. The site currently treats them as one audience ("For boards, staff, and industry audiences"). Consider whether the service description should acknowledge the range or stay broad.
