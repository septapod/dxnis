## Notes for Brent

1. **The Brewery CU case study is anonymized.** I described it as "a $180M credit union" to protect client confidentiality. If Brewery is OK being named, even better. Named case studies convert 3x better than anonymous ones (Hinge data).

2. **The bio is longer than before.** The research says this is correct for a solo consultant. The guide section needs to establish both empathy ("I've been in your world") and authority ("here's what I've done"). The bio earns its length by being specific, not by being long.

3. **"Book a Strategy Call" is the CTA.** Research says action verb + specific deliverable outperforms vague CTAs. "Let's Talk" sounds like a first date. "Book a Strategy Call" sounds like the beginning of something useful.

4. **Three testimonials, not seven.** Quality over quantity. Ray, Nancy, and Mary Beth are the strongest because they name specific outcomes or frame Brent's unique value. The others can live on a future testimonials page if the site grows to multiple pages.

5. **The humor is absent from this draft.** Website copy is a different format than newsletter writing. The voice rules still apply (direct, concrete, alongside the reader) but the build-build-drop rhythm and satirical humor work better in long-form. The website needs to convert in 30 seconds, not entertain for 10 minutes. If you want humor injected somewhere, tell me where and I'll take a shot.

6. **The problem cards are the riskiest copy on the page.** They name specific pain. If they land, they stop the scroll. If they feel presumptuous, they push people away. These need your gut check more than anything else on the page.
